Who am I?

I’m a creative person in pretty much all that I set out to do, and an artist in many senses of the word. I have always loved singing and dancing and have taken these interests with me through years of school and summer camp. I have also always had a passion for visual arts starting when I was an elementary school student, turning book reports into intricate artistic masterpieces which eventually lead to becoming an art major in college.

But I never considered myself to be a “free-spirit artist.” I like to follow a choreographed dance routine and I like to sketch with a pencil and eraser before any paint reaches the canvas. I like to map out my ideas and I like to have a plan, I love notebooks (especially those that have floral designs on the cover) and I have a guilty pleasure for writing lists. I always knew that I was an interesting mix of the right and left brains, but I never realized that I could turn this dichotomy into a profession. Then I stumbled across UX design.

Let's backtrack for a second.

I went to a small university and majored in Studio Art. I took classes in painting, drawing, 3D design, graphic design and color theory, among many others. I went through numerous career ideas while in undergrad, from architect to interior designer to art director to exhibition designer. Following my college graduation I was an exhibition design intern at the American Museum of Natural History in New York City where I was able to combine design with the human experience in a way that I hadn’t been exposed to before. But as much as I loved living my own personal Night at the Museum, I felt more removed from my ‘end user’ than I initially thought I would. What I wanted was to be more of a hands-on designer, working closely with my clients, listening to and solving design challenges that affect and influence people every day.

The epiphany:

I discovered companies like IDEO and Frog Design and I became fascinated with innovation design consultancies and the notion of human centered design. I didn’t quite know what this world was all about, but I knew that I wanted to be a part of it. I finally settled on the field of UX design after finding that I shared a common love with the people at these agencies of crafting complete and satisfying user experiences. I enrolled in Bloc’s Designer Track program where I am learning UX and UI design along with the fundamentals of frontend development, and I haven’t looked back since.

I am utilizing both my right and left brains every day by becoming well versed in user research, user experience and visual design. I am excited by all that this field has to offer, and I am looking forward to future opportunities.
